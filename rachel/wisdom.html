And finally:

I wrote this in an essay at the very end of my senior year of high school. I had no idea what I wanted to do with my life. Reading it today, I think I made some good choices. 17-year-old Rachel would be proud.


“You see, I don’t know what I want to do with my life. Going into college, I feel myself pulled in a thousand directions. What should I study? English? Business? Journalism? It’s overwhelming.

At the same time, though, I know exactly what I want to do. I’ve learned in this class that the repeated process of stepping outside the box and stretching my limits leads to the ultimate complexity of fulfillment that I’m searching for. Where ideas intersect, life becomes more than the sum of its parts. I want to “make connections” for the rest of my life, because for me, there is meaning in that--it’s a map through the network of allegorical caves that lie ahead. Everything is connected if you look hard enough-- what matters is seeking out those intersections, and making them mean something to you. This class has taught me to seek knowledge, then apply it to what I already know, regardless of what career I choose.

This year, in this class, I’ve adopted a life goal that accepts the infinite: to seek connections everywhere, and apply the meaning I find to my own life. I’ve learned how to learn, and I’ve learned how to love it, and that’s something that will follow me beyond the walls of Westview.”
