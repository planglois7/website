defining "strategist"

I've heard lots of definitions that try to explain what a strategist actually is. I'm collecting them here:

  1. “A strategist is a hybrid thinker that brings structure to chaos.” -Erika Bridges + Saied Vahidi
  2. “A strategist wears many hats.” -Natalie Kim
  3. “A strategist finds the real, honest problem to solve.” -Brock Kirby
  4. “A strategist is, most simply, an ethnographer.” -Kunal Merchant
  5. “A strategist uses creativity and research. They have to be critical and proactive. They help people understand.” -Alex Morrison

So, how do I define it? What is a strategist to me? I think my answer will change, but for now, I see this job as a way to get paid to build and communicate an informed opinion. That’s pretty incredible.

my specialties

I’ve heard many people list the sub-roles of a strategist: all the things you might find yourself doing on the job at some point. This is what Natalie Kim meant when she said a strategist wears “many hats.” Here are just a few of my favorites.

  1. Creative sparring partner:

    This is one of my favorites, but also one of the hardest parts about the advertising industry. As a strategist on my NSAC team, I’ve experienced quite a few “sparring matches” with creatives: productive but occasionally heated conversations about the best way to move a project forward. Learning the skills to advocate for my insights while encouraging creativity is one of my biggest lifelong learning tasks on the horizon.


  2. Futurologist:

    Alex Morrison inspired me with his “shit you should Google” list: a daily trend check-in to stay on top of our constantly shifting world and culture. I love the idea of being on the forefront of social and industrial change. I want to know what’s going on, and this industry encourages -- even requires -- the pursuit of the next big thing.


  3. “Numbers person”:

    I was totally “math girl” in high school. AP Calc, AP Stats -- I did it all. Though I chose to indulge my right brain in college, I still have a knack for logic and numbers. I love the idea of digging through data, then distilling it to make a clear, compelling case for a bigger concept.


  4. Cultural anthropologist:

    Anthropology: my other high school nerd obsession. My senior year, I wrote a 30-page ethnography comparing my all-girls a cappella group and our all-boys counterpart. I argued that we were low-level cult-like organizations and it was a blast (ask me about my sorority membership, too. . .). Basically, I am deeply interested in the way people live, work and interact, and I love that strategy is deeply linked with that field of study.
